Restrictions on the number of objects a container can receive.

Many counting problems can be reduced to thinking about how to assign a set of objects to containers under various constraints. The Twelvefold Way was introduced by Gian-Carlo Rota and classifies a variety of common counting problems. This was further extended by Kenneth P. Bogart to the Twentyfold Way, which is represented below. This calculator determines how many ways to assign objects to containers based on the criteria: Are the objects/containers distinguishable? How many objects can each container hold? Does the order of the objects within the container matter?
